Overview

Marjoram is an aromatic herb in the mint family, closely related to oregano. It is native to the Mediterranean region and has small, oval leaves and tiny white or pink flowers. Marjoram has a sweet, floral flavor with hints of pine and citrus and is commonly used fresh or dried as a culinary herb.

Cultural Overlap

Marjoram is used in cuisines around the world, particularly in Mediterranean and European cuisines. It is used to flavor meats, stews, soups, sauces, and salads, as well as baked goods and herbal teas. Marjoram also has cultural significance in traditional medicine and folklore, where it is believed to have various health benefits and protective properties.
