Overview

Fenugreek is an herbaceous plant in the Fabaceae family, native to the Mediterranean region and Western Asia. Its seeds and leaves are used as culinary ingredients and have a slightly bitter, nutty flavor. Fenugreek seeds are small, yellowish-brown, and angular, with a distinctive aroma.

Cultural Overlap

Fenugreek is used in cuisines around the world, particularly in Indian, Middle Eastern, and North African cuisines. It is used as a spice in curries, stews, and soups and is also used to flavor bread, pickles, and spice blends. Fenugreek leaves are used fresh or dried in cooking and have a similar flavor to the seeds. Fenugreek also has cultural significance in traditional medicine and is believed to have various health benefits.
