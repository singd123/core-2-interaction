Overview

Asafoetida, also known as "devil's dung" due to its strong smell, is a resinous gum derived from the roots of several species of Ferula, a perennial herb native to Iran and Afghanistan. It has been used for centuries as a culinary spice, particularly in Indian and Middle Eastern cuisines, where it adds a unique flavor to dishes.

Cultural Overlap

Asafoetida is widely used in Indian cuisine, particularly in vegetarian dishes and pickles. It's also common in Middle Eastern cooking, where it's used to flavor meats and lentil dishes. Additionally, it has historical significance in traditional medicine systems such as Ayurveda and traditional Chinese medicine.
