Overview

Dill is an aromatic herb in the Apiaceae family, native to the Mediterranean region and Southwest Asia. It has feathery green leaves and small yellow flowers and is prized for its delicate flavor, which is reminiscent of anise and celery. Dill is commonly used fresh or dried as a culinary herb.

Cultural Overlap

Dill is used in cuisines around the world, particularly in European, Middle Eastern, and Scandinavian cuisines. It is used to flavor dishes such as soups, salads, fish, pickles, and sauces. Dill also has cultural significance in traditional medicine and folklore, where it is believed to have various health benefits and protective properties.
