Overview

Cumin is a spice derived from the seeds of the cumin plant, native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia. It has a warm, earthy flavor with hints of citrus and is commonly used in cooking and baking. Cumin seeds are small, elongated, and ridged, with a distinctive aroma.

Cultural Overlap

Cumin is widely used in cuisines around the world, particularly in Indian, Middle Eastern, Mexican, and North African cuisines. It is used whole or ground to add flavor to dishes such as curries, stews, soups, and rice dishes. Cumin also has cultural significance in traditional medicine and rituals in many cultures.
