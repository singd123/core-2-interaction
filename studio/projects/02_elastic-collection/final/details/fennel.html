Overview

Fennel is a flowering plant in the carrot family, native to the Mediterranean region. It has feathery green leaves and yellow flowers, and its seeds, leaves, and bulb are all used as culinary ingredients. Fennel has a sweet, licorice-like flavor and is prized for its aromatic and medicinal properties.

Cultural Overlap

Fennel is used in cuisines around the world, particularly in Mediterranean, Indian, and Middle Eastern cuisines. It is used in both savory and sweet dishes, such as salads, soups, curries, sausages, and desserts. Fennel seeds are also used in traditional medicine for their digestive and therapeutic properties.
