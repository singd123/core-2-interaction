Overview

Cardamom is a spice derived from the seeds of plants in the ginger family, native to India and Southeast Asia. There are two main types of cardamom: green cardamom and black cardamom. Green cardamom, also known as true cardamom, has a sweet, floral flavor with hints of citrus and mint, while black cardamom has a smoky, earthy flavor.

Cultural Overlap

Cardamom is widely used in Indian, Middle Eastern, and Scandinavian cuisines. It is a key ingredient in Indian desserts, masala chai, and savory dishes such as biryani. In Middle Eastern cuisine, cardamom is used in coffee and sweets. In Scandinavian baking, cardamom is used to flavor breads and pastries. Additionally, cardamom has cultural significance in traditional medicine and rituals in many cultures.
