Overview

Cayenne pepper is a hot chili pepper derived from the Capsicum annuum plant, native to Central and South America. It is named after the city of Cayenne in French Guiana. Cayenne pepper is known for its intense heat and vibrant red color, and it is commonly used as a spice to add heat to dishes.

Cultural Overlap

Cayenne pepper is used in cuisines worldwide, particularly in Caribbean, Mexican, and Southeast Asian cuisines. It is used in sauces, marinades, and spice blends to add heat and flavor to dishes. Cayenne pepper also has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, including pain relief and improved digestion.
