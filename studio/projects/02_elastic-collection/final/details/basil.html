Overview

Basil is a fragrant herb belonging to the mint family. It has a sweet, aromatic flavor with hints of pepper, mint, and anise. Basil is native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa and is cultivated worldwide for its culinary and medicinal uses.

Cultural Overlap

Basil plays a prominent role in Mediterranean, Thai, and Italian cuisines. It's commonly used in pasta sauces, pesto, salads, and soups. Additionally, basil has cultural significance in various religious and spiritual practices, symbolizing love, protection, and fertility.
