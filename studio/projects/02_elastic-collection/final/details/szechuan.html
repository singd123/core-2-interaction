Overview

Sichuan pepper is a spice derived from the husks of the seeds of several species of Zanthoxylum, native to China and East Asia. It has a unique flavor profile with citrusy, floral, and numbing properties, distinct from black pepper or chili peppers. Sichuan pepper is commonly used in Sichuan cuisine and other regional Chinese cuisines.

Cultural Overlap

Sichuan pepper is a key ingredient in Sichuan cuisine, where it is used to flavor dishes such as mapo tofu, hot pot, and Sichuan peppercorn chicken. It is also used in other regional Chinese cuisines, as well as in Tibetan, Nepali, and Japanese cuisines. Sichuan pepper has cultural significance in Chinese medicine and folklore, where it is believed to have various health benefits and protective properties.
