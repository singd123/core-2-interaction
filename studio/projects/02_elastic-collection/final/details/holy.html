Overview

Holy basil, also known as tulsi, is a sacred herb in Hinduism and Ayurvedic medicine. It is native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia and is prized for its medicinal properties and spiritual significance. Holy basil has a strong, aromatic flavor with hints of clove and pepper and is used fresh or dried as a culinary herb and tea.

Cultural Overlap

Holy basil is revered in Hindu culture and is often grown in homes and temples for its protective and purifying properties. It is used in religious rituals, ceremonies, and festivals and is believed to bring blessings and good fortune. Holy basil is also used in Ayurvedic medicine for its potential health benefits, including stress relief, immune support, and respiratory health.
