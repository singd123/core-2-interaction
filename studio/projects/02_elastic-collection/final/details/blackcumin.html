Overview

Black cumin refers to two different plants with similar names but distinct characteristics. Bunium bulbocastanum, also known as black cumin seed, is a plant native to Europe and Asia, whose seeds are used as a spice in cooking. Nigella sativa, commonly known as black seed or black cumin, is a flowering plant native to Southwest Asia, whose seeds are also used as a spice and have medicinal properties.

Cultural Overlap

Black cumin seeds are used in various cuisines, including Indian, Middle Eastern, and North African cuisines, to add a nutty, peppery flavor to dishes. Nigella sativa seeds are particularly common in Middle Eastern and South Asian cuisines, where they are used as a spice and a remedy for various ailments. Additionally, black cumin seeds have historical significance in ancient Egyptian culture, where they were found in the tomb of King Tutankhamun.
