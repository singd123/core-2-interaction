Overview

Cinnamon is a spice derived from the inner bark of trees in the Cinnamomum genus, native to Sri Lanka (Cinnamomum verum) and China (Cinnamomum cassia). It has a sweet, warm flavor with hints of citrus and spice. Cinnamon is one of the oldest known spices, valued for its aromatic and culinary properties.

Cultural Overlap

Cinnamon is widely used in cuisines around the world, including Indian, Middle Eastern, European, and American cuisines. It is used in sweet and savory dishes, such as desserts, curries, and baked goods. Cinnamon also has cultural significance in religious rituals and ceremonies, particularly in Hindu and Buddhist traditions.
