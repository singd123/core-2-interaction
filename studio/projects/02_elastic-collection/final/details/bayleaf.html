Overview

Bay leaves are aromatic leaves commonly used in cooking to add flavor to soups, stews, and sauces. They come from the bay laurel tree, native to the Mediterranean region. Bay leaves have a slightly bitter taste and a strong, pungent aroma when dried.

Cultural Overlap

Bay leaves are utilized in various cuisines around the world, including Mediterranean, Indian, and Caribbean cuisines. They are often used in soups, stocks, and braised dishes. Additionally, bay leaves hold cultural significance in ancient Greek and Roman traditions, where they were used as symbols of honor and victory.
