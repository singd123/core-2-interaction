Overview

Lavender is a flowering plant in the mint family, native to the Mediterranean region, Africa, and Asia. It has fragrant purple flowers and silvery-green leaves and is prized for its aromatic and medicinal properties. Lavender has a sweet, floral flavor with hints of citrus and spice and is commonly used as a culinary herb and tea.

Cultural Overlap

Lavender is used in cuisines around the world, particularly in Mediterranean and French cuisines. It is used to flavor desserts, baked goods, beverages, and savory dishes such as meats, sauces, and salads. Lavender also has cultural significance in traditional medicine and aromatherapy, where it is believed to have calming and therapeutic properties.
